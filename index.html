  • Dual-Mode Transformable Drone (Drive & Fly Mode)
    Designed and prototyped an innovative drone capable of both aerial flight and terrestrial movement. The drone features a SolidWorks-modeled wheeled chassis with integrated fixed propellers and a belt-driven locomotion mechanism for ground mode. Actuation systems enable transformation between drive and fly modes, with real-time control via a Teensy 4.0 microcontroller and a 16-channel Radiomaster transmitter-receiver. Structural integrity was validated using Ansys simulations, and the body was fabricated with carbon fiber PLA using 3D printing techniques.
  • Optimization of Front Disc Brake Plate (Yamaha FZ S Fi) Using Ansys
    Conducted thermal and structural analysis of a front disc brake for the Yamaha FZ S Fi motorcycle using Ansys. Various high-performance materials were evaluated—Stainless Steel, Cast Iron, Ti-6Al-4V, and 6061-T6 Aluminum. Based on mechanical and thermal stress criteria, Stainless Steel was selected. Topology optimization led to a 16.4% weight reduction while maintaining structural integrity, demonstrating advanced simulation and design optimization capabilities.
